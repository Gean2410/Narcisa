Slave's Profile

When I half-open my eyes to the advancing light,
Beating back the shadow and perfidious lethargy,
I see beyond the discreet translucency
Of the white curtain, a child in sight.

Pupil like a gentle, living gazelle,
With serene fear, gathering the ardor,
Forehead immersed in pallor... Laugh of innocence,
Laugh that now brings anguish, now brings hope...

She's the fleeting sketch of a living statue,
With arms outstretched, looming in the shadow,
Silent, attentive, contemplative!

Statue? No, for this foolish chain
Must break, wretched, captive,
This motherly affection, hidden by her mistress!

- Narcisa Amália, in "Nebulae". 1872.

The Lake

Calm, deep, translucent, vast the lake,
Far off, quivering, it trembled away,
On its clear mirror, the foliage lay,
Curved, from a forest, offering shade and grace.

Earth and sky, undulating, in the cold
Canvas merged! The vague lament that flowed
Through water, it seemed, from both was told,
Unhindered, ululating, endless, forebode!

"A common piece of a remote and rugged place,"
perhaps, but all the charm it did embrace
you would feel; its beauty you'd contemplate;
with me, you'd hear its silence that speaks,
and you'd imbibe in the freshness it keeps

all the vital breath of Nature's estate!

-Narcisa Amalia, in "Nebulae". 1872,

XX

My inspiring angel lacks upon her cheeks
The coraline tints of the morning's grace,
Nor do her lips echo the lively songs
Of the indigenous maiden paid!

On her resplendent brow, there's no burden
Of a crown bedecked with marvels untold,
And she doesn't steal from the drifting mist
Its clear, flowing mantles.

My inspiring angel is cold and somber
Like the sun that reddens the polar sky!
Her pale countenance betrays her, unlike
The harsh contemplation of the past!

She carries on her head a wreath of yearning,
In her languid gaze, there's a morbidity's touch;
She wears the rugged cloak of tempests wild,
And her name is Sorrow!


-Narcisa Amalia, in "Nebulae". 1872,

Poem souvenir

Do you still remember, Adelaide dear,
Our cherished childhood's bright frontier?
That blissful time, so full of cheer,
And the sun so fair, crystal clear?

You were like a little flower,
Timidly unfurling your grace,
You, a white lily of the dell,
Blossomed in your stem's small cell,
Radiating golden rosy embrace.

Our sky held no clouds in sight,
No dawn refused to shine its light,
No nymphs in water played their part,
No gentle breeze touched the heart,
Without bringing forth delight.

You shared your dreams, so pure and bright,
Of immaculate purity, a guiding light,
Aromatic verses, a harmonious dance,
You were a syllable of divine romance.

And within our beings, completely,
Love, unfading, so sweet and neat,
How existence would gently smile,
With each prayer we'd reconcile,
Innocence's vows, in prayer's retreat.

-Narcisa Amalia, in "Nebulae". 1872,

Resignation

In the silence of fragrant nights,
When the wave, weeping, kisses the shore,
Beneath the trembling brilliance of the stars,
I bow my sad, fainting forehead.
And I see the passing of chaste shadows
From the deliriums of joyous youth;
I clutch my heart shattered
By the cruel grip of longing.
How sweet is the memory of that time
When the ground of existence was covered in flowers,
When the murmurs of the spheres sang
The tempting verses of love!
I soared joyfully over rugged hills
Chasing after colorful butterflies...
The vault of Elysium was so pure,
Hanging over dewy meadows!...
Today, laughter burns my lips with madness,
The fiery gleam in my eyes is feverish:
My voice only echoes with plaintive sighs,
Thorny brambles line my path.
But what does this pain that overwhelms me matter,
That separates me from the boisterous songs,
When on the gentle wings of poetry,
I am lifted to more beautiful realms?!...
From the blue sky, from the flowers, from the wandering mist,
From fantastical beings, from fragrances,
Regions full of enchantment have been created for me,
Illuminated by soft flames of golden light!
In the silence of fragrant nights,
When the wave, weeping, kisses the shore,
She teaches me to pray, timid and believing,
She warms the fading hope within me.
Oh! Blessed is this pain that weighs me down,
That separates me from the noisy chants,
From afar, I see the throngs that deliriously rejoice,
And lose themselves in winding detours!...

- Narcisa Amália, in "Nebulae". 1872.

To a Poet

Thou art the star that illuminates and guides
The dark path of my thoughts;
Thou art the voice that consoles and creates
New illusions in my torment.

Thou art the genius that inspires and inflames
With love for the homeland, for holy freedom;
Thou art the bard who lends me fame
And gives me life in the harmony that sings.

Thou art the angel that guards and watches over
The perils of life on this journey;
Thou art the friend who supports and cares
For my blessed fortune.

Thou art everything that binds me to the earth,
That gives me strength to resist;
Thou art the light that illuminates and encloses
The secret of my existence.

- Narcisa Amália, in "Nebulae". 1872.

Aspirations

The blue flashes of your eyes
Awaken hope in the soul;
Grant joys, oh sweet child,
Celestial flower in a world of thorns!

Now you lean your forehead in reverie,
Weary of games, content,
Emil, beautiful innocent dreamer,
Paints fantasies in the colors of the prism;

Now you fly lightly among lilies,
Shaking radiance, little angel;
And the zephyr sends you a caress,
While the stars offer you tender embraces!...

But if a white pearl, shed from your glistening lashes,
Clings to your face,
From your lips, in melodious speech,
Rain down sublime idylls!

From your mouth, like a ruby pomegranate,
Fall a thousand happy, saintly kisses!
Your wings of beautiful hues,
Ah! Do not tear them on the path of vice!

- Narcisa Amália, in "Nebulae". 1872.

VOTE

The turning breeze that plays so sweetly
Among the palm tree's leaves,
Bring to your soul sacred inspiration,
Enchanting delights.

The graceful flower that joyfully unfolds
In the mottled soil,
Envies the tranquil and happy life
Of children always loved.

Only your eyes shed sweet tears
Of mystical tenderness;
Like light nymphs, may joys surround you,
May happiness embrace you!

May all your children obediently scatter
Roses along your path;
And may thorns bow beneath the steps
Of the adored Mother!

Such are the prayers sent to the heavens
By your humble daughter;
And God welcomes the incense, even if it emanates
From a white marvel!

- Narcisa Amália, in "Nebulae". 1872.

SADNESS

My inspiring angel lacks upon the cheeks
The coraline hues of the morning;
Nor on the lips are the lively songs
Of the indigenous maiden paid!

Not burdened on the dazzling forehead
With a crown of splendor and wonders,
Nor does it steal from the floating mist
The clear veils.

My inspiring angel is cold and sad
Like the sun that reddens the polar sky!
Betrayed by the pallid countenance - of sorrow
The bitter contemplation!

Carries on the head a theme of longing,
In the languid gaze, there's a morbidity;
Wears the ermine cloak of storms,
And is called Sadness!

- Narcisa Amália, in "Nebulae". 1872.

FRAGMENTS

My soul is like the cooing dove,
That sighs, flutters, gasps, and weeps
In the shadowy foliage of the mango tree;
Like a gentle swan with silvery plumes,
That succumbs to love upon the foamy waves,
Uttering the final lament!

My heart is the lotus of the East,
Fainting at the languors of the West,
Begging for the dew's milky pearls;
And in the pale twilight, it bends,
Whispering as it rolls over the meadow,
"Oh breeze, carry me to cerulean shores!"

Alas! In the gardens of youth,
I wish to forget the brambles of existence,
To turn the bitter pains into nectar;
Then... I will ascend to paradise,
Having lilies of smiles on my lips,
On the wings of mystical loves!

- Narcisa Amália, in "Nebulae". 1872.
